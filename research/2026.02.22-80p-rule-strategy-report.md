# 80% Rule (80P) Strategy Report

**Date:** 2026-02-22
**Data:** 259 RTH sessions (Feb 20, 2025 - Feb 20, 2026) — NQ/MNQ 1-min volumetric bars
**Method:** Replay-based optimization across 160 configurations

---

## 1. Strategy Overview

The 80% Rule (Dalton, *Markets in Profile*) is a mean-reversion setup triggered when price opens outside the prior session's Value Area and fails to sustain the breakout:

1. **Setup condition:** RTH open prints outside prior session's Value Area (VAH or VAL)
2. **Entry trigger:** Price re-enters VA and is "accepted" — 1 consecutive 30-min close inside VA
3. **Direction:** LONG when open < VAL (gap down fails); SHORT when open > VAH (gap up fails)
4. **Target:** Opposite VA boundary (full traverse)
5. **Stop:** Beyond the VA boundary that was breached + 10pt buffer

### Relationship to Gap Fills

The 80P is fundamentally a **gap fill strategy with an acceptance confirmation filter**. Analysis of 259 sessions confirms:

- 100% of accepted 80P setups had the RTH gap fill during the session
- 0 accepted setups occurred without a gap fill
- 78% of accepted setups achieved full VA traverse
- The acceptance filter eliminates fake gap fills (wick-and-reverse patterns)

## 2. Value Area Computation

VA is calculated using the **CBOT standard POC expansion method**:
1. Build volume profile by distributing each bar's volume across all price bins from bar low to bar high
2. Find POC (price bin with maximum volume)
3. Expand contiguously outward from POC, adding the higher-volume side at each step
4. Stop when 70% of total session volume is captured
5. The resulting contiguous range = Value Area (VAL to VAH)

## 5. Key Findings

### 5.1 ETH VA Outperforms RTH VA
ETH (overnight + RTH) VA captures overnight institutional positioning. The "open outside VA" condition is more meaningful against ETH VA because it accounts for overnight price discovery.

### 5.2 Single-Period Acceptance (acc=1) Beats Double (acc=2)
With 259 sessions, faster entry (1 x 30-min close inside VA) produces more trades and better total returns than the stricter 2-period requirement. The stricter filter improves WR slightly but loses too many setups.

### 5.3 High R Targets Are Optimal
The best configurations use 3R-4R targets with ~37-40% WR. The few big winners (especially during volatility events like April 2025 tariff crash: +$4,514 and +$5,954 trades) more than compensate for frequent small stops.

### 5.4 BOTH Directions Outperform LONG-Only at High R
SHORT trades capture crash/correction events that produce outsized winners. At conservative R targets (1R, 1.5R), LONG-only is safer. At 3R+ targets, including shorts is essential for capturing tail events.

### 5.5 Composite VA (3-day, 5-day) Underperforms
Wider composite VAs produce larger stops, worse R:R, and lower WR. Single-session VA (RTH or ETH) is optimal.

## 6. Loss Diagnosis — Why WR is Low Despite High VA Traversal

The paradox: **78% of accepted 80P setups traverse the full VA** during the session, yet win rate is only 37-45%. A forensic replay of all 60 trades (eth_1d, acc=1, BOTH) reveals the root cause.

### 6.1 The Problem: Entry Model, Not Stop Tightness

The primary edge leak is **entry location within the Value Area**, not stop placement.

| Metric | LONG Trades | SHORT Trades |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Mean entry location (0=VAL, 1=VAH) | **0.32** (near VAL edge) | **0.32** (near VAH edge) |
| **Winners** enter at | **0.45** (deeper into VA) | **0.37** (deeper into VA) |
| **Losers** enter at | **0.23** (barely inside edge) | **0.26** (barely inside edge) |

The 30-min acceptance bar triggers as soon as price barely pokes inside the VA — a close just above VAL (for longs) or just below VAH (for shorts). The stop sits right behind that edge (VAL/VAH - 10pt buffer), giving only **~40-60 pts of risk** against a VA that averages **210 pts wide**. Price re-tests the VA boundary after acceptance, and those edge entries get stopped out on the retest.

**Winners enter 45% into the VA; losers enter at 23%.** The deeper the entry, the more room from the stop and the closer to target.

### 6.2 Exit Reason Breakdown (opposite_va target, 60 trades)

**Half of all trades get stopped out.** But the critical question is: were those stops premature?

### 6.3 Stop-Out Forensics — Were Stops Too Tight?

Of the 30 stopped trades:

| Metric | Count | % |
|--------|-------|---|
| Price LATER reached target (opposite VA) | **6** | **20%** |
| Price NEVER reached target | **24** | **80%** |

**Only 20% of stopped trades were "fixable" with wider stops.** The other 80% were correctly stopped — the setup genuinely failed and price never came back to the target. This means:

- **The stop model is NOT the primary issue** — 80% of stops were legitimate failures
- **The entry model is the issue** — we're taking setups where the VA re-entry is shallow and uncommitted

### 6.4 What Stopped Trades Look Like Before the Stop

| MFE Before Stop Hit | Value |
|---------------------|-------|
| 25th percentile | 6 pts |
| **Median** | **11 pts** |
| 75th percentile | 21 pts |
| Trades with < 5pt MFE | 7 (23%) |

Stopped trades barely move in the favorable direction at all — **median MFE of only 11 pts** before the stop hits. Nearly a quarter of stopped trades never get more than 5 pts in their favor. These are not trades where price "almost worked" — they're setups where price rejected immediately upon re-entering the VA.

### 6.5 Stop Hit Speed

| Bars Before Stop | Value |
|------------------|-------|
| Mean | 33 bars (33 min) |
| **Median** | **16 bars (16 min)** |
| 25th percentile | 8 bars |

Half the stopped trades die within 16 minutes. The acceptance bar fires, we enter, and the market immediately reverses back through the VA boundary.

### 6.6 Wider Stops Don't Help (opposite_va target)

| Stop Buffer | WR | $/Month |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| 10pt (current) | 33.3% | $24 |
| 20pt | 33.3% | -$163 |
| 30pt | 35.0% | -$309 |
| 60pt | 38.3% | -$238 |
| 85pt | 41.7% | -$87 |
| 110pt | 41.7% | $125 |

Wider stops barely improve WR and **destroy P&L** because the extra risk per trade isn't compensated by enough saved trades. This confirms the stop isn't the problem.

### 6.7 The VA Traversal Paradox Explained

The 78% full VA traversal stat is measured at the **session level** — did price touch both VAH and VAL at any point during the day? Yes, 78% of the time. But this includes:

- Price traversing the VA **before** our entry (during IB or pre-acceptance)
- Price traversing the VA **after** our stop was hit
- Price traversing the VA in a choppy, non-linear fashion

Our **trade-level** MFE tells the real story:

| MFE Milestone | % of trades reaching it |
|---------------|------------------------|
| >= 0.5R | 53% |
| >= 1.0R | 35% |
| >= 2.0R | 20% |
| >= 4.0R | 8% |

**Median trade MFE is only 0.5R.** Over half the trades never even reach 1R favorable. The strategy works because the top 8% of trades reach 4R+ and produce outsized winners.

## 12. Implementation Notes

### Strategy Parameters (Recommended Production Config)
```
VA Source:        ETH 1-day (full session including overnight)
Acceptance:       1 x 30-min close inside VA (acc=1)
Directions:       BOTH (LONG + SHORT)
Target:           4R (4x risk distance from entry)
Stop:             VA boundary + 10pt buffer
Entry cutoff:     13:00 ET (no new entries after)
Exit cutoff:      15:30 ET (force close)
Min VA width:     25 pts
```

### Engine Considerations
- 80P trades are **exempt from PM VWAP breach exits** — the strategy is mean-reversion, not trend-following, so VWAP alignment is irrelevant
- Stop and target levels handle all exits (plus EOD force-close)
- The strategy should track VA acceptance state across 30-min TPO periods starting from IB end (10:30 ET)

## 13. Conclusion

The 80P Rule is a **viable standalone strategy** producing ~$986/month per account with the optimal configuration. Its edge comes from:

1. **Structural mean-reversion** — failed VA breakouts revert at statistically significant rates
2. **Acceptance filter** — eliminates fake gap fills and wick reversals
3. **Fat-tail capture** — high R targets (4R) harvest outsized moves during volatility events
4. **Frequency** — 5.1 trades/month provides enough volume for meaningful income

The strategy is **not** the 80% WR / 1:4 R:R setup originally hypothesized. Real-world performance is closer to **37% WR / 3:1 R:R** — a classic trend-following P&L distribution applied to a mean-reversion entry signal.
