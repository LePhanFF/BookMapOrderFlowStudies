**Date:** 2026-02-22
**Data:** 259 RTH sessions (Feb 20, 2025 - Feb 20, 2026) — NQ/MNQ 1-min volumetric bars
**Method:** Replay-based optimization (160 configs) + forensic loss diagnosis (60 trades)

---

## 1. Strategy Overview

The 80% Rule (Dalton, *Markets in Profile*) is a mean-reversion setup triggered when price opens outside the prior session's Value Area and fails to sustain the breakout:

1. **Setup condition:** RTH open prints outside prior session's Value Area (VAH or VAL)
2. **Entry trigger:** Price re-enters VA and is "accepted" — 1 consecutive 30-min close inside VA
3. **Direction:** LONG when open < VAL (gap down fails); SHORT when open > VAH (gap up fails)
4. **Target:** 4R (4x the risk distance from entry)
5. **Stop:** Beyond the VA boundary that was breached + 10pt buffer
6. **Exit cutoff:** 15:30 ET force close

### Relationship to Gap Fills

The 80P is fundamentally a **gap fill strategy with an acceptance confirmation filter**. Analysis of 259 sessions confirms:

- 100% of accepted 80P setups had the RTH gap fill during the session
- 0 accepted setups occurred without a gap fill
- 78% of accepted setups achieved full VA traverse (session level)
- The acceptance filter eliminates fake gap fills (wick-and-reverse patterns)

## 2. Value Area Computation

VA is calculated using the **CBOT standard POC expansion method**:
1. Build volume profile by distributing each bar's volume across all price bins from bar low to bar high
2. Find POC (price bin with maximum volume)
3. Expand contiguously outward from POC, adding the higher-volume side at each step
4. Stop when 70% of total session volume is captured
5. The resulting contiguous range = Value Area (VAL to VAH)

## 5. Key Findings

### 5.1 ETH VA Outperforms RTH VA
ETH (overnight + RTH) VA captures overnight institutional positioning. The "open outside VA" condition is more meaningful against ETH VA because it accounts for overnight price discovery.

### 5.2 Single-Period Acceptance (acc=1) Beats Double (acc=2)
Faster entry (1 x 30-min close inside VA) produces more trades and better total returns than the stricter 2-period requirement. The stricter filter improves WR slightly but loses too many setups.

### 5.3 BOTH Directions Outperform LONG-Only at High R
SHORT trades capture crash/correction events that produce outsized winners. At conservative R targets (1R, 1.5R), LONG-only is safer. At 3R+ targets, including shorts is essential for capturing tail events.

### 5.4 Composite VA (3-day, 5-day) Underperforms
Wider composite VAs produce larger stops, worse R:R, and lower WR. Single-session VA (RTH or ETH) is optimal.

## 6. Loss Diagnosis — Why WR is Low Despite High VA Traversal

**The paradox:** 78% of accepted 80P setups traverse the full VA during the session, yet the production config only wins 37% of trades. A forensic replay of all 60 trades reveals the root cause.

### 6.1 The Problem is the Entry Model, Not Stop Tightness

The primary edge leak is **entry location within the Value Area**.

| Metric | LONG Trades | SHORT Trades |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Mean entry location (0=VAL edge, 1=VAH edge) | 0.32 (near VAL) | 0.32 (near VAH) |
| **Winners** enter at | **0.45** (deeper into VA) | **0.37** (deeper into VA) |
| **Losers** enter at | **0.23** (barely inside edge) | **0.26** (barely inside edge) |

The 30-min acceptance bar triggers as soon as price barely pokes inside the VA. The stop sits right behind the VA edge (VAL/VAH - 10pt buffer), giving only ~40-60 pts of risk against a VA averaging 210 pts wide. Price re-tests the VA boundary after shallow acceptance, and those edge entries get stopped out.

**Winners enter 45% into the VA; losers enter at 23%.** Deeper entry = more room from stop, closer to target.

### 6.2 Exit Reason Breakdown

| Exit Reason | Count | % | WR | Avg P&L | Avg MFE | Avg MAE |
|------------|-------|---|-----|---------|---------|---------|
| TARGET | 8 | 13.3% | 100% | $2,283 | 244p | 33p |
| **STOP** | **33** | **55.0%** | **0%** | **-$434** | **28p** | **50p** |

**55% of trades get stopped out.** 32% exit at EOD (mostly profitable — heading toward target but ran out of time). Only 13% reach the 4R target.

### 6.3 Stop-Out Forensics: 80% of Stops Were Legitimate Failures

Of the 33 stopped trades:

| Metric | Count | % |
|--------|-------|---|
| Price LATER reached 4R target | 8 | 24% |
| Price NEVER reached 4R target | **25** | **76%** |

**Only 24% of stopped trades were "fixable" with wider stops.** The other 76% were correctly stopped — the setup genuinely failed and price never reached target. This confirms the stop model is sound; the entry model is what lets bad setups through.

### 6.6 Wider Stops Don't Help

Wider stops barely improve WR and **destroy P&L** because extra risk per trade isn't compensated by enough saved trades.

### 6.7 The VA Traversal Paradox Explained

The 78% full VA traversal stat is measured at the **session level** — did price touch both VAH and VAL at any point during the day? But our **trade-level** MFE tells the real story:

| MFE Milestone | % of trades reaching it |
|---------------|------------------------|
| >= 0.5R | 65% |
| >= 1.0R | 43% |
| >= 2.0R | 25% |
| >= 3.0R | 22% |
| >= 4.0R (target) | **13%** |

**Only 13% of trades reach the 4R target.** The VA traverses during the session, but often before our entry, after our stop, or in a choppy non-linear path that hits our stop en route to the opposite boundary. The strategy profits because those 13% are worth $2,283 average vs -$434 average loss.

### 6.8 Potential Entry Improvements

The diagnosis points to specific improvements:

1. **Require deeper VA penetration** — Don't enter until price is 35-40% into the VA, not at the edge (23%). This reduces trade count but dramatically improves WR
2. **Delta confirmation at entry** — Winners tend to have confirming delta (positive for LONG, negative for SHORT) at the acceptance bar
3. **IB context filter** — Skip setups where the IB already expanded away from the VA (breakout day, not reversion day)
4. **Time filter** — First acceptance after IB (bar 29-59) performs differently than later entries; segment and filter

```
Directions:       BOTH (LONG + SHORT)

```

### Engine Considerations
- 80P trades are **exempt from PM VWAP breach exits** — the strategy is mean-reversion, not trend-following
- Stop and target levels handle all exits (plus EOD force-close)
- The strategy tracks VA acceptance state across 30-min TPO periods starting from IB end (10:30 ET)

## 13. Conclusion

### What This Strategy IS
The 80P Rule is a **low win-rate, high reward-to-risk mean-reversion strategy** producing ~$986/month on the production config. Its P&L distribution resembles trend-following: frequent small losses punctuated by outsized winners during volatility events.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Win rate | **37%** |
| Avg winner / Avg loser | **3.0x** |
| Profit factor | **1.74** |
| Monthly return | **$986** |
| Trade frequency | **5.1/month** |

### What This Strategy is NOT
- It is NOT an 80% win rate strategy (the name is misleading)
- It is NOT a 67% WR strategy (that was a cherry-picked conservative config with 1.3 trades/month)
- It is NOT a strategy where wider stops fix the WR problem (80% of stopped trades were legitimate failures)

### Why the Win Rate is Low
The forensic diagnosis reveals the **entry model** is the primary issue:
1. **Acceptance triggers at the VA edge** — entries at 23-32% into the VA, not deep inside
2. **Stop is right behind the edge** — only 40-60pt of room on a 210pt-wide VA
3. **Price re-tests the boundary** after shallow acceptance, stopping out edge entries
4. **Winners enter deeper** (45% into VA) — they have more room and shorter distance to target

### Edge Source
Despite the low WR, the strategy is profitable because:
1. **Structural mean-reversion** — failed VA breakouts revert at statistically significant rates
2. **Acceptance filter** — eliminates fake gap fills and wick reversals
3. **Fat-tail capture** — 4R targets harvest outsized moves during volatility events (April 2025: +$10,468 in 2 trades)
4. **Asymmetric payoff** — avg winner is 3x avg loser, so 37% WR is sufficient for positive expectancy

### Next Steps for Improvement
1. **Entry improvement:** Require deeper VA penetration (35-40% into VA) before triggering entry
2. **Delta filter:** Require confirming order flow delta at acceptance bar
3. **IB context:** Skip setups where IB expands away from VA (breakout, not reversion)
4. **Hybrid target:** Use opposite_va on narrow VA days, R-multiple on wide VA days
