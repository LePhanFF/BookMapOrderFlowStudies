## How the Study Was Conducted (Methodology)

### Data Pipeline

```
Raw CSV (NQ 1-min volumetric bars, 354,387 rows)
  → load_csv('NQ')                     # data/loader.py
  → filter_rth()                       # data/session.py → 99,514 RTH bars
  → compute_all_features()             # data/features.py → adds IB, delta, VWAP, FVG, BPR, day_type
  → Per-session pre-computation        # IB POC, POC shape, VWAP position, C-period
  → IB edge touch detection            # Post-IB bar-by-bar scan
  → Acceptance model evaluation        # 2x1min, 2x5min, 30min, delta
  → Backtest with stop/target logic    # Walk-forward, no lookahead
```

### Per-Session Pre-Computation (259 sessions)

For each session:

1. **IB bars** = first 60 1-min bars (9:30-10:30 ET)
2. **IB High/Low/Range/Mid** = max(high), min(low), H-L, (H+L)/2
3. **IB POC** = Point of Control computed via `calculate_value_area()` on IB bars only (volume profile with tick_size=0.25, va_percent=0.70)
4. **POC Shape Classification:**
   - P_SHAPE: POC in upper third of IB range (bullish skew)
   - B_SHAPE: POC in lower third (bearish skew)
   - D_SHAPE: POC in center third (neutral)
5. **VWAP at IB close** = VWAP value at bar 60
6. **C-period analysis** = bars 60-89 (10:30-11:00), classify close relative to IB
7. **EOD day type** = from `compute_all_features()` based on full-session extension
8. **Balance day definition** = max EOD extension < 0.5x IB range
9. **Wide balance** = balance + IB range > 200 pts

### IB Edge Touch Detection

Post-IB (bar 60 onward), scan every 1-min bar for:

- **IBH touch:** `bar.high >= IBH - 5 pts` (within 5 points of IBH)
- **IBL touch:** `bar.low <= IBL + 5 pts` (within 5 points of IBL)
- **Cooldown:** Minimum 15 bars between same-edge touches (prevents counting noise as multiple touches)
- **Entry cutoff:** No touches detected after 14:00 ET

For each touch, record:
- Penetration depth (how far past the edge)
- Delta at touch bar (positive/negative)
- Volume spike (vs rolling average)
- VWAP position relative to IB mid
- BPR/FVG presence at touch
- Touch number (1st, 2nd, 3rd...)

### Acceptance Model Evaluation

For each touch, look at the next 60 bars and check:

1. **2x1min:** 2 consecutive 1-min closes back inside IB
2. **2x5min:** 2 consecutive 5-bar period closes back inside IB (i.e., bars 5 and 10 close inside)
3. **1x30min:** The bar 30 bars after the touch closes inside IB
4. **Delta-only:** Delta at touch bar aligns with fade direction (positive delta at IBL = buy signal)

### Fade Success Definition

**Success = price reaches IB midpoint within 60 bars of the touch.** This is the raw "reached_mid" metric used to evaluate filters before running full backtests.

### Backtest Mechanics

For each entry model configuration:

1. **Entry price** = close of the acceptance bar (not the touch bar — avoids lookahead)
2. **Stop** = IB edge ± 10% of IB range (e.g., IBL - 0.10 * IB_range for LONG)
3. **Target** = IB midpoint (primary), VWAP, dynamic based on POC shape, or 2R
4. **Walk remaining bars** after entry:
   - If `bar.low <= stop` (LONG) or `bar.high >= stop` (SHORT) → STOP out
   - If `bar.high >= target` (LONG) or `bar.low <= target` (SHORT) → TARGET hit
   - If `bar.time >= 15:30` → EOD exit at close
5. **P&L** = (exit - entry) * direction
6. **Dollar P&L** = P&L_pts * $2.00/pt * 5 contracts - (slippage + commission) * 2 sides * 5 contracts
   - Slippage: 0.50 pts per side ($1.00)
   - Commission: $1.24 per contract per side
   - Total cost per trade: $22.40 for 5 MNQ

### No Lookahead Bias

- Entry occurs AFTER acceptance is confirmed (not at the touch)
- Day type classification uses EOD extension (not used as entry filter — see Part 1.3)
- VWAP is the current developing VWAP at touch time (computed cumulatively)
- All future bar checks walk forward from the entry bar

## Conclusions

### What We Proved

1. **B-day LONG at IBL is the highest-edge day-type-specific strategy: 76% WR, PF 5.89.** Long at the low of the B-shape works because the heavy volume node at the bottom provides structural support.

2. **NEVER short P-days (29% WR, -$477/mo).** P-days are bullish skew — price pushes above IBH and stays. Shorting fights the day's thesis AND NQ's structural long bias.

3. **First touch + LONG + 30min acceptance = 82% WR, PF 9.35.** The highest quality combination found in any study. "Fade the first test" + time confirmation + NQ long bias = near-guaranteed mean reversion.

4. **Neutral days fade both directions equally well (69% combined, $1,610/mo).** The only day type where SHORT keeps up with LONG (72% vs 66%).

5. **B-day SHORT ("shorting the seam") works but is inferior to LONG (69% vs 76%).** Take the short only if you missed the LONG.

6. **30-min acceptance naturally excludes trend days (zero trades).** The filter is self-correcting — you don't need to predict the day type.

7. **P-day LONG opportunities are rare but profitable.** Only 5 trades with 30min acceptance. P-shape LONG (any day type) is a better proxy at 65% WR, $990/mo.

8. **IB POC shape (b-shape, P-shape, D-shape) is identifiable at 10:30** and provides useful directional bias, unlike EOD day type which can't be predicted (20-32% accuracy).

### Recommended Strategy: Tiered Approach

#### Tier 1: B-Day IBL LONG (Primary)


## Part 10: DPOC Migration as Real-Time Entry Filter

**Key Question:** Does DPOC (Developing Point of Control) migration direction at the time of an IB edge touch improve the win rate of the fade entry?

### Methodology

DPOC migration is computed at each touch bar using 30-minute volume-weighted POC slices (same methodology as `rockit-framework/modules/dpoc_migration.py`). For each touch we record:

- **Direction** (up/down/flat) — net DPOC migration since first post-IB slice
- **Velocity** — average pts per 30-min slice (last 3 slices)
- **Regime** — trending_strong, trending_fading, stabilizing, transitional
- **Retention %** — how much of peak migration is preserved at entry time
- **Alignment** — DPOC direction vs fade direction (aligned = both bullish or both bearish)

**8,350 level touches** enriched with DPOC data (7,777 valid, 573 insufficient). Detection across IBH, IBL, IB_VAH, IB_VAL, PRIOR_VAH, PRIOR_VAL.

### Distribution at Touch Points

| DPOC Direction | Count | % |
|---------------|-------|---|
| Up | 2,875 | 34% |
| Down | 2,200 | 26% |
| Flat | 1,654 | 20% |
| Insufficient | 1,048 | 13% |

| DPOC Regime | Count | % |
|-------------|-------|---|
| Stabilizing | 3,816 | **46%** |
| Transitional | 2,238 | 27% |
| Trending fading | 553 | 7% |
| Trending strong | 122 | 1% |

**Key observation:** 46% of all IB edge touches occur when DPOC is **stabilizing** — confirming these are predominantly balance/rotation days.

### Headline Results

#### DPOC Direction Alignment (All Day Types, IBL/IBH + 30min Acceptance)

| Filter | Level | Dir | N | WR | PF | $/Mo |
|--------|-------|-----|---|-----|-----|------|
| No DPOC filter (baseline) | IBL | LONG | 183 | 58% | 1.20 | $565 |
| DPOC aligned (up/flat) | IBL | LONG | 69 | 55% | 0.90 | -$131 |
| **DPOC contra (down)** | **IBL** | **LONG** | **114** | **60%** | **1.47** | **$696** |
| No DPOC filter (baseline) | IBH | SHORT | 229 | 46% | 0.96 | -$158 |
| DPOC contra (up) | IBH | SHORT | 155 | 46% | 1.04 | $104 |

**Surprising finding:** DPOC "contra" (DPOC migrating *against* the fade direction) **outperforms** DPOC "aligned." For LONG at IBL, DPOC migrating DOWN produces 60% WR vs 55% aligned. This makes sense: when DPOC migrates down to IBL, you're buying *at the developing value* — the POC has come to you.

#### DPOC Regime Filter

| Regime | IBL LONG WR | IBL LONG $/Mo | IBH SHORT WR | IBH SHORT $/Mo |
|--------|-------------|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| Trending fading | **86%** (N=7) | $64 | 50% | -$107 |
| **Stabilizing** | **60%** | **$421** | 47% | -$68 |
| Transitional | 49% | $16 | 43% | $145 |

**Stabilizing regime** is the workhorse: most trades (N=124/155 for IBL/IBH) and consistent positive edge for longs. Trending fading shows 86% WR but tiny sample.

#### DPOC × Day Type — Incremental Edge

| Day Type | Filter | Level | N | WR | PF | Delta vs Base |
|----------|--------|-------|---|-----|-----|--------------|
| **Neutral** | **Stabilizing** | **IBL LONG** | **35** | **71%** | **1.67** | **+9pp** |
| B-day | DPOC aligned | IBL LONG | 13 | 69% | 3.28 | +3pp |
| B-day | Stabilizing | IBH SHORT | 32 | 66% | 2.76 | +2pp |
| P-day | Stabilizing | IBH SHORT | 55 | 33% | 0.52 | +7pp (still bad) |

**Best incremental value:** Neutral day + stabilizing DPOC + IBL LONG = **71% WR (+9pp over base)**. The stabilizing regime confirms the day is rotating, making mean-reversion fades more reliable.

#### DPOC Velocity Buckets

| Velocity | IBL LONG WR | IBH SHORT WR |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Low (<4 pts/30min) | 57% | 49% |
| **Medium (4-10)** | **65%** | 41% |
| High (>10) | 51% | 49% |

**Medium velocity (4-10 pts/30min)** is the sweet spot for IBL LONG — enough movement to confirm value building, not so fast that it's a trend day.

#### DPOC Retention

| Retention | IBL LONG WR (N) | IBL LONG PF |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| High (>70%) | 60% (N=174) | 1.26 |
| **Low (<40%)** | **22% (N=9)** | **0.48** |

**Low DPOC retention = exhaustion = avoid.** If the DPOC has given back most of its migration, the directional move is exhausted and fading that edge becomes dangerous.

### DPOC Migration Verdict

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Does DPOC direction alignment improve edge fades? | **No — contra is slightly better** |
| Does DPOC regime matter? | **Yes — stabilizing is the workhorse** |
| Best DPOC filter for LONG at IBL? | **Stabilizing regime + medium velocity** |
| Best incremental edge? | **Neutral + stabilizing = +9pp WR** |
| Should you filter OUT with DPOC? | **Yes — avoid low retention (<40%) and trending_strong** |
| Is DPOC migration a primary filter? | **No — it's a secondary/confirmation filter** |

### Practical Takeaway

DPOC migration is **not a primary entry filter** but serves as a useful **regime confirmation**:

1. **Stabilizing DPOC regime** confirms balance day rotation — safe to fade IB edges
2. **Low retention (<40%)** = red flag — skip the trade (22% WR)
3. **Medium velocity (4-10 pts/30min)** is the sweet spot for IBL longs
4. **Don't filter by DPOC direction alignment** — counter-intuitively, contra outperforms
5. **Strongest combo:** Neutral day + stabilizing DPOC + IBL LONG = 71% WR
